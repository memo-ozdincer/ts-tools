1. PROBLEM STATEMENT & PHYSICAL CONTEXT
================================================================================

GOAL: Given a molecular geometry near a transition state (TS), find the nearest
local energy MINIMUM on the potential energy surface (PES).

PHYSICAL CONTEXT:
  - Transition states are saddle points on the PES (one imaginary frequency)
  - Minima have all positive vibrational frequencies (n_neg == 0)
  - The optimizer must navigate from a noisy starting geometry (near the TS)
    to the closest minimum, crossing regions of mixed curvature

TWO METHODS IMPLEMENTED:
  1. Fixed-step gradient descent:
       x_{k+1} = x_k - alpha * grad E(x_k)
                = x_k + alpha * forces(x_k)

  2. Newton-Raphson (the focus of all optimization work):
       x_{k+1} = x_k - H(x_k)^{-1} * grad E(x_k)
                = x_k + H(x_k)^{-1} * forces(x_k)
     The inverse Hessian is computed via pseudoinverse in the vibrational
     subspace to avoid singularities from translation/rotation modes.

Both support optional Eckart projection of the gradient to prevent
translation/rotation drift.

CONVERGENCE CRITERION:
  - Strict: n_neg == 0 (zero negative vibrational eigenvalues)
  - SPDN:   n_neg_reliable == 0 AND force_norm < threshold
            (ignores ghost eigenvalues below tau_soft)

WHY THIS IS HARD:
  - The PES has regions of negative curvature near the TS
  - Small negative eigenvalues ("ghost modes") are numerically ambiguous
  - The Hessian can be ill-conditioned (condition numbers > 10^6)
  - Trust-radius dynamics can collapse, rendering steps useless
  - Eigenvector rotation between steps makes mode-tracking unreliable

2. DATASET & EXPERIMENTAL SETUP
================================================================================

DATASET: Transition1x
  - Source:  Official dataset (Schreiner et al.)
  - Format:  HDF5 file (~1-2 GB)
  - Content: Small organic molecules (H, C, N, O)
  - Level:   wB97x/6-31G(d) DFT reference energies/forces
  - Splits:  train, val, test
  - Per-sample: reactant geometry, TS geometry, product geometry, atomic
                numbers, DFT energy, DFT forces, reaction SMILES, formula

CALCULATOR: DFTB0 (Density Functional Tight-Binding, zero-order)
  - Semi-empirical quantum mechanics method
  - ~2-3 orders of magnitude faster than full DFT
  - Provides forces AND Hessians (needed for Newton-Raphson)
  - Implemented via SCINE/Sparrow backend
  - CPU-only, no GPU needed
  - Can describe bond breaking/formation

STARTING GEOMETRY CONSTRUCTION:
  The starting geometry is constructed from the dataset sample:

  Base geometries:
    "reactant"       → batch.pos_reactant
    "ts"             → batch.pos_transition
    "midpoint_rt"    → 0.5 * pos_reactant + 0.5 * pos_transition
    "three_quarter_rt" → 0.25 * pos_reactant + 0.75 * pos_transition

  Noise application (e.g., "midpoint_rt_noise2.0A"):
    1. Parse: base = "midpoint_rt", noise_level = 2.0 Angstroms
    2. Seed: torch.manual_seed(noise_seed + sample_index)
       → Each sample gets unique but reproducible noise
    3. Noise: isotropic Gaussian, per-atom, std = noise_level
       noise = torch.randn_like(coords_flat) * noise_rms_angstrom
    4. Apply: noisy_coords = coords_flat + noise

  v4 SETUP: "midpoint_rt_noise2.0A", seed=42, 30 samples
  v5 SETUP: sweep noise {0.5, 1.0, 1.5, 2.0}A × seeds {42,123,456,789,1024}
            × 50 samples

PARALLELIZATION:
  - ProcessPoolExecutor via ParallelSCINEProcessor
  - N_WORKERS = TOTAL_CPUS / THREADS_PER_WORKER
  - Default: 192 CPUs / 4 threads = 48 workers
  - Each worker runs one sample independently

3. ALGORITHM EVOLUTION (v1 through v5)
================================================================================

--- v1: Basic Newton-Raphson ---

  Step: delta_x = -H^{-1} * grad
  Computed via eigendecomposition in vibrational subspace.
  Hard filter: exclude modes with |lambda| < nr_threshold.
  Trust region: cap max per-atom displacement at max_atom_disp.

  Parameters:
    nr_threshold:  8e-3 (eigenvalue cutoff)
    max_atom_disp: 0.3 -> 1.3 A

--- v2: Diagnostics & LM Damping ---

  New features:
  1. Cascading evaluation:
     n_neg counted at 8 thresholds: [0, 1e-4, 5e-4, 1e-3, 2e-3, 5e-3, 8e-3, 1e-2]
     Logged every step. Enables post-hoc threshold relaxation analysis.

  2. Levenberg-Marquardt damping:
     step_i = (g . v_i) * |lambda_i| / (lambda_i^2 + mu^2)
     Smooth alternative to hard filter. Activated when lm_mu > 0.

  3. Two-phase threshold annealing:
     Bulk phase: use nr_threshold
     Cleanup phase: drop to cleanup_nr_threshold when force < anneal_force_threshold

  4. Spectral gap diagnostic:
     |lambda_min| / |lambda_second_min| on vibrational eigenvalues
     Logged every step. Detects dominant-mode vs multi-mode negative curvature.

  5. Full bottom-K vibrational spectrum logged every step.

--- v3: Shifted Newton & Stagnation Escape ---

  New features:
  1. Shifted Newton step:
     step_i = (g . v_i) / (lambda_i + sigma)
     where sigma = max(0, -lambda_min) + shift_epsilon
     Makes all effective eigenvalues positive.
     Negative modes get LARGER weights → more aggressive along problematic
     directions. This was the key breakthrough for v3.

  2. Stagnation-triggered negative-mode perturbation:
     When n_neg unchanged for stagnation_window steps AND negative eigenvalues
     are small, apply displacement along negative eigenvectors.

  3. Adaptive LM mu annealing:
     Reduce mu when close to convergence (few small negative eigenvalues).

  4. Negative-mode line search:
     Scan along negative eigenvector to find where curvature changes sign.

  5. Trust-region fixes:
     Higher floor (0.01 A), reset after escape, gradient-norm based minimum
     step size.

  6. Per-step diagnostic logging:
     - Gradient-mode overlap for negative modes: |g . v_i| / |g|
     - Step-mode decomposition: fraction along neg vs pos eigenspace
     - Stagnation counter
     - Energy plateau detection

  RESULT: Shifted Newton epsilon=5e-4 achieved 80% convergence (12/15)
          on a 15-sample test. Best v3 config.

--- v4: Addressing Three Failure Populations ---

  From v3 analysis, three failure populations identified:

  Population 1 — "Blind modes" (samples 000, 010, 007, 003):
    5 near-zero negative eigenvalues, gradient overlap ≈ 0.001 for all modes.
    NR step component ∝ (g . v_i) ≈ 0 → optimizer literally blind to these modes.

  Population 2 — "Trust-crushed" (samples 005, 002, 009, 004, 001):
    n_neg = 1-3, moderate gradient overlap (0.13-0.16), but trust radius at
    floor (0.01 A) kills the already-small neg-mode step component.

  Population 3 — "True saddle" (sample 012):
    Large |lambda_min| ≈ 0.148, gradient orthogonal to both negative modes.

  v4 Features (all default OFF for backward compatibility):

  1. SEPARATE NEG-MODE TRUST RADIUS (--neg-trust-floor):
     Decomposes NR step into neg/pos eigenvalue subspace components.
     Independent trust radii. Neg-mode floor default 0.05 A.
     Target: Pop 1+2 (prevents trust collapse from crushing neg-mode steps)

  2. BLIND-MODE CORRECTION (--blind-mode-threshold, --blind-correction-alpha):
     For neg modes with |g . v_i|/|g| < threshold, adds fixed-magnitude
     perturbation with alternating sign per step. Zero extra evaluations.
     Target: Pop 1

  3. AGGRESSIVE TRUST RECOVERY (--aggressive-trust-recovery):
     Softer trust shrink (x0.5 instead of x0.25) near convergence.
     Auto-recovery: resets trust to 0.5*max when n_neg decreases.
     Target: Pop 2

  4. BIDIRECTIONAL STAGNATION ESCAPE (--escape-bidirectional):
     Probes BOTH +/- v_0 for most negative mode, evaluates Hessian at both,
     picks direction with less negative eigenvalue. 2-3 extra evals.
     Target: Pop 1+2

  5. MODE-FOLLOWING (--mode-follow-eval-threshold, --mode-follow-alpha):
     For large |lambda_min|: bidirectional probe along most negative
     eigenvector. 2 extra evals per event. Only after --mode-follow-after-steps.
     Target: Pop 3

  RESULT: All v4 features HURT performance. See Section 4.

--- v5: SPDN (Spectrally-Partitioned DIIS-Newton) ---

  Complete rethink. See Section 8 for full design rationale.

  New features:
  1. Spectral step builder: bounded weights (max 1/tau_hard = 100x)
  2. GDIIS (Geometry DIIS): breaks oscillation cycles
  3. Backtracking line search: replaces trust region for SPDN mode
  4. Modified convergence: ignores ghost eigenvalues, requires force convergence
  5. Polyak heavy-ball momentum (optional)
  6. Comprehensive spectral conditioning diagnostics

5. FAILURE MODE ANALYSIS (AUTOPSY FINDINGS)
================================================================================

FAILURE CLASSIFICATION METHODOLOGY:
  Each failed trajectory is classified by examining:
  - Final eigenvalue state (n_neg, min_vib_eval, bottom_spectrum)
  - Eigenvalue trajectory (improving, oscillating, stuck)
  - Energy trajectory (decreasing, plateau, oscillating)
  - Gradient-mode overlap (can optimizer address negative modes?)
  - Stagnation (n_neg unchanged for how long?)
  - Trust radius history (collapsed to floor?)
  - Eigenvector continuity (modes rotating?)

FAILURE MODES:

  1. "oscillating" (~70% of failures):
     Trust radius collapses to floor (0.01 A), then thousands of useless
     micro-steps dominated by positive-mode components while negative
     eigenvalues bounce around near zero.

     ROOT CAUSE: Period-8 oscillation cycle.
     The shifted Newton step with ε=5e-4 amplifies eigenvalues near zero
     by up to 2000x. When |lambda| ≈ 5e-4, weight = 1/(lambda + 5e-4)
     ≈ 1/1e-3 = 1000. The resulting large step overshoots, causing the
     trust region to shrink. At the TR floor (0.01 A), the actual step
     is dominated by the capped displacement, which is mostly along
     positive modes. The negative modes see almost no progress.

  2. "almost_converged" (~12% of failures):
     Very close to minimum: |min_eval| < 2e-3, n_neg ≤ 3.
     Would converge with eigenvalue tolerance or more steps.

  3. "ghost_modes" (~3% of failures):
     All negative eigenvalues in [-1e-4, 0) band. No eigenvalues below
     -1e-4. Gradient overlap ≈ 0 for all ghost modes. Eigenvectors
     rotate between steps (overlap < 0.5). These are numerically-zero
     eigenvalues that the optimizer cannot address.

  4. "energy_plateau" (rare):
     Energy stopped decreasing but negative eigenvalues remain.

  5. "genuinely_stuck" (0% in v4):
     Never observed. The optimizer always makes SOME progress.

  6. "slow_convergence" (rare):
     Eigenvalues improving but too slowly to converge within step budget.

THREE FAILURE POPULATIONS (from detailed analysis):

  Population A — "Ghost modes":
    - Affected samples: 000, 010, 007, 003
    - Characteristics: n_neg = 5-12 but ALL eigenvalues ≈ -1e-6 to -1e-8
    - Gradient overlap: ≈ 0.001 for all negative modes
    - Eigenvector continuity: modes rotate every few steps (overlap < 0.5)
    - Physical interpretation: these eigenvalues are below numerical noise
    - DIAGNOSIS: not a real saddle point, just numerical noise

  Population B — "Trust-crushed":
    - Affected samples: 005, 002, 009, 004, 001
    - Characteristics: n_neg = 1-3, eigenvalues -0.001 to -0.019
    - Gradient overlap: moderate (0.13-0.16)
    - Trust radius: at floor (0.01 A) for >75% of steps
    - Step decomposition: 95%+ along positive modes, <5% along negative
    - DIAGNOSIS: optimizer CAN see the negative modes but trust radius
      kills the step before it can make progress

  Population C — "Border cases":
    - Small negative eigenvalues that fluctuate around zero
    - Would converge with patience or slight tolerance
    - Not a fundamental failure, just insufficient steps/budget

OSCILLATION CYCLE ANATOMY:

  The dominant failure mode (oscillating, 70%) follows a specific pattern:

  Step N:   eigenvalue λ ≈ -0.005, weight = 1/(0.005 + 5e-4) = 182x
            Step size: 0.15 A (after trust cap)
            Trust radius: 0.15 A

  Step N+1: Overshoot. Energy increased. Trust shrinks to 0.15 * 0.25 = 0.0375 A
            λ shifted to -0.003

  Step N+2: Smaller step works. Trust stays or shrinks further.
            λ oscillates back to -0.006

  Step N+3: Weight now 1/(0.006 + 5e-4) = 154x. Large step again.
            Overshoot. Trust shrinks to floor (0.01 A).

  Steps N+4 to N+∞: Trust at floor. Max displacement 0.01 A.
            Step mostly along positive modes (where |g . v_i| is large).
            Negative-mode component: 0.01 * (small_overlap) ≈ 0.0001 A.
            Eigenvalue change per step: negligible.
            STUCK.

  This is a PERIOD-8 oscillation (detected by FFT autocorrelation in
  the surface forensics analysis). The period varies slightly but 8 is
  the mode.

6. EIGENVALUE TOLERANCE INVESTIGATION
================================================================================

The eigenvalue tolerance investigation (analyze_nr_eigenvalue_justification.py)
was designed as an EXPERIMENT, not a post-hoc justification. It produces
evidence FOR or AGAINST tolerance filtering.

KEY QUESTION: Can we declare convergence when all eigenvalues are above
-T (for some threshold T) instead of strictly above 0?

SIX ANALYSIS MODULES:

A1. Band Evolution Statistics
  - When do ghost eigenvalues first appear?
  - Do real negative modes shrink into the ghost band, or do ghost modes
    appear de novo?
  - Answer: Ghost modes tend to appear in the final 10-20% of the
    trajectory, after the optimizer has already resolved the large
    negative eigenvalues.

A2. Tolerance Sweep Experiment
  - For each tolerance T in [0, 1e-5, 5e-5, 1e-4, ..., 1e-2]:
    - How many failed trajectories would become "converged"?
    - How many converged trajectories would have stopped EARLY (danger)?
  - CRITICAL FINDING: At T = 1e-4, the false convergence rate was 97%.
    That is, 97% of trajectories that would be declared converged under
    T = 1e-4 later breach the tolerance. The eigenvalues do NOT stay
    above -T; they oscillate through it.

A3. Physical Significance Analysis
  - Estimate Hessian numerical noise floor from stagnant regions
  - Compare ghost eigenvalue magnitudes to noise floor
  - Result: ghost eigenvalues (|λ| < 1e-4) are at or below the
    numerical noise floor of the DFTB0 Hessian

A4. Stability / False Convergence Detection
  - Simulate: if tolerance T applied at first qualifying step S,
    do eigenvalues stay within tolerance at steps S+1, S+5, S+50, S+500?
  - Result: EXTREMELY UNSTABLE. Ghost modes appear and disappear
    unpredictably. Eigenvectors rotate. The same eigenvalue index can
    be positive at step N and negative at step N+1.
  - 97% false convergence rate at T = 1e-4 (most promising threshold)

A5. Cascade Gap Analysis
  - Per failed sample: required tolerance T = |min_final_eval|
  - CDF of required tolerances across all failed samples
  - Finding: NO natural gap in the CDF. The required tolerance
    distribution is continuous from 1e-8 to 1e-1. There is no clean
    separation between "ghost modes" and "real saddle points."

A6. Eigenvalue-Force-Energy Consistency Check
  - For tolerance-qualifying geometries: are forces also small?
  - Result: INCONSISTENT. Many tolerance-qualifying geometries have
    force norms >> force_converged threshold. They are NOT at minima
    even if eigenvalues look OK.

CONCLUSION FROM TOLERANCE INVESTIGATION:

  Eigenvalue tolerance filtering is NOT a viable convergence strategy.

  Evidence AGAINST:
  - 97% false convergence rate at the most promising threshold
  - No natural gap in required tolerance distribution
  - Ghost modes are unstable (rotate, appear/disappear)
  - Force-eigenvalue inconsistency: tolerance-qualifying != at minimum

  Evidence FOR (limited):
  - Ghost eigenvalues are below Hessian noise floor (physically meaningless)
  - Would increase measured convergence rate by 20-30%

  VERDICT: The convergence problem must be solved by fixing the OPTIMIZER
  (preventing oscillation-collapse), not by relaxing the convergence
  CRITERION (eigenvalue tolerance).

  However, for SPDN mode specifically, a combined criterion is used:
    n_neg_reliable == 0 AND force_norm < threshold
  This is different from pure eigenvalue tolerance because:
  1. Only ignores eigenvalues below tau_soft (1e-4), which are below noise
  2. Requires FORCE convergence as a second gate
  3. This combination prevents the 97% false convergence problem

7. SURFACE FORENSICS FINDINGS
================================================================================

The surface forensics analysis (analyze_nr_surface_forensics.py) examines
the potential energy surface behavior and optimizer dynamics.

F1. Trust Radius ↔ Eigenvalue Correlation
  - Strong negative correlation: as trust radius decreases, n_neg tends
    to remain constant or increase
  - Trust collapse events (>50% drop in one step) are followed by
    eigenvalue oscillations in the subsequent 10-20 steps

F2. Eigenvalue Band Transition Matrices
  - 8x8 transition matrix (from_band → to_band per step)
  - High self-transition rates for extreme bands (strongly positive stays
    positive, strongly negative stays negative)
  - Ghost band [-1e-4, 0) has LOW self-transition: modes enter and leave
    frequently. Residence time ≈ 3-5 steps.

F3. Stagnation Anatomy
  - Stagnation windows (>50 steps, same n_neg):
    - Energy range: < 1e-6 eV (flat)
    - Force norm: slowly increasing (optimizer losing progress)
    - Eigenvalue range: oscillating with constant n_neg
    - Trust radius: at floor for 100% of stagnant steps
    - Displacement: < 0.001 A per step (effectively frozen)

F4. Oscillation Cycle Detection (FFT-based)
  - Dominant period: 8 steps (most common)
  - Detected in 70% of failed trajectories
  - Oscillation in: n_neg (alternating 0 and 1-3), trust_radius,
    and min_vib_eval

F5. Distance Evolution
  - Converged trajectories: move 0.5-2.0 A from start to minimum
  - Failed trajectories: move 0.3-1.5 A then STOP (trust-crushed)
  - Failed trajectories are NOT drifting away — they're stuck near
    a point that is close to but not exactly at the minimum

F6. Ghost Mode Characterization
  - Eigenvectors of ghost modes rotate with overlap < 0.5 between steps
  - The same physical direction is NOT persistently negative
  - Different modes take turns being in the ghost band
  - Correlates weakly with molecule size (more atoms → more modes
    near zero → more ghost modes)

F7. Trust-Crushed Population
  - When TR at floor (0.01 A):
    step_along_neg_frac ≈ 2-5% (most of the step is in positive space)
    Actual neg-mode displacement: ~0.0002-0.0005 A
    At this rate, moving 0.01 A along negative modes takes 20-50 steps
    but eigenvalues don't improve because the curvature changes


================================================================================
8. THE SPDN ALGORITHM (v5 DESIGN RATIONALE)
================================================================================

SPDN = Spectrally-Partitioned DIIS-Newton

Designed to address the three root causes identified in Sections 5-7:
  1. Oscillation-collapse cycle (70% of failures)
  2. Trust-radius crushing of negative-mode steps
  3. Ghost mode false-negatives

--- Component 1: Spectral Step Builder ---

  Instead of uniform 1/(lambda + sigma) weighting (shifted Newton),
  partition eigenvalues into three reliability regimes:

  HARD regime (|lambda| > tau_hard, default 0.01):
    weight = 1 / |lambda|
    Full Newton behavior. These eigenvalues are reliable.

  SOFT regime (tau_soft < |lambda| <= tau_hard):
    weight = 1 / tau_hard
    Capped at 1/tau_hard = 100x (vs shifted Newton's 2000x)
    Provides progress without explosive amplification.

  NULL regime (|lambda| <= tau_soft, default 1e-4):
    weight = 1 / tau_hard
    Ghost modes get same bounded weight as soft modes.
    Not zeroed out (still contributes), but cannot dominate.

  Implementation:
    abs_lam = torch.abs(lam_all)
    effective_lam = torch.clamp(abs_lam, min=tau_hard)
    weights = 1.0 / effective_lam
    coeffs = V_all.T @ grad
    nr_step = V_all @ (coeffs * weights)
    delta_x = -nr_step

  KEY DIFFERENCE FROM SHIFTED NEWTON:
    Shifted Newton: max weight = 1/shift_epsilon = 1/5e-4 = 2000x
    SPDN:           max weight = 1/tau_hard = 1/0.01 = 100x
    This 20x reduction prevents the oscillation-collapse cycle.

--- Component 2: GDIIS (Geometry DIIS) ---

  GDIIS = Geometry Direct Inversion of the Iterative Subspace.
  Standard technique in computational chemistry for breaking oscillation.

  Stores M recent (geometry, gradient) pairs.
  Solves: min ||sum_i c_i * e_i||^2 subject to sum_i c_i = 1
  where e_i is the gradient at geometry i.

  Linear system:
    [B   1] [c]   [0]
    [1^T 0] [λ] = [1]

  where B_ij = e_i . e_j (error overlap matrix)

  Extrapolated geometry: x_diis = sum_i c_i * x_i
  This finds the point where the oscillation is centered — the "average"
  of the oscillating geometries, which is often close to the minimum.

  Parameters:
    max_size:  8 (stored geometry-gradient pairs)
    min_size:  3 (minimum before attempting extrapolation)
    diis_every: 5 (attempt every 5 steps)

  Acceptance criteria:
    1. Coefficient validation: max |c_i| <= 10 (reject if ill-conditioned)
    2. Geometry validation: min interatomic distance satisfied
    3. Energy validation: E(x_diis) <= E(x_current) (Armijo-like)

  If accepted: jump to DIIS geometry and continue.
  If rejected: ignore and continue from current geometry.

--- Component 3: Backtracking Line Search ---

  Replaces the trust region for SPDN mode.

  Armijo condition: f(x + alpha*d) <= f(x) + c1 * alpha * grad.dot(d)
  where c1 = 1e-4 (sufficient decrease constant)

  Algorithm:
    alpha = 1.0 (start with full step)
    for i in range(15):
      trial = x + alpha * direction
      if min_interatomic_dist violated: alpha *= 0.5; continue
      trial_energy = predict_fn(trial)
      if Armijo satisfied: ACCEPT
      alpha *= 0.5

  WHY BETTER THAN TRUST REGION:
  - Trust region shrinks based on quadratic model accuracy
  - When Hessian is inaccurate (near ghost modes), the model is always
    inaccurate, so TR always shrinks → collapse to floor
  - Backtracking line search only needs ENERGY decrease, not model accuracy
  - More robust to Hessian noise

--- Component 4: Modified Convergence ---

  SPDN convergence:
    n_neg_reliable = count(evals_vib < -tau_soft)
    converged = (n_neg_reliable == 0) AND (force_norm < force_converged)

  This ignores eigenvalues in [-tau_soft, 0) (ghost modes) BUT requires
  force convergence as a safety gate. This prevents the 97% false
  convergence problem identified in the tolerance investigation.

--- Component 5: Polyak Heavy-Ball Momentum (Optional) ---

  delta_x = delta_x + momentum * (x_current - x_previous)

  Dampens high-frequency oscillations. Default momentum = 0.0 (off).
  Tested values: {0.0, 0.2, 0.4}

--- Spectral Conditioning Diagnostics ---

  Every SPDN step logs detailed spectral information:
  - Partition counts: n_hard_pos, n_hard_neg, n_soft, n_null
  - Condition numbers: cond_full, cond_hard_only
  - Eigenvalue ranges: eval_range, eval_spread_positive, eval_spread_negative
  - Change rates: eval_change_rate_mean, eval_max_change, eval_change_rate_hard
  - Extremes: min_pos_eval, max_neg_eval

--- Saddle Point Diagnostic (DIAGNOSTIC ONLY) ---

  At each step, detect saddle-like configurations:
    is_saddle_like = (force_norm < 0.01) AND (n_neg_reliable > 0)

  This is PURELY diagnostic — it does not affect optimization decisions.
  Logged for learning about the PES landscape.

9. THE OVERFITTING PROBLEM & v5 EXPERIMENTAL REDESIGN
================================================================================

PROBLEM IDENTIFIED:

  The v4 grid search used 30 fixed samples with noise_seed=42.
  Every run produced the EXACT SAME 30 starting geometries.

  Result: the same 16 samples always converged, the same 7 never did,
  and 7 were borderline. We weren't testing optimizers — we were
  overfitting to 30 specific starting points.

  The v4 features (ntf, blind correction, etc.) made things worse
  because:
  1. The 16 "easy" samples converge with plain SN anyway
  2. The 7 "impossible" samples have starting geometries too far from
     any minimum for ANY optimizer to reach
  3. The 7 "borderline" samples are sensitive to hyperparameters, but
     with only one noise realization we can't distinguish signal from noise

  Furthermore, 2.0 A per-atom Gaussian noise is ENORMOUS. Typical bond
  lengths are 1-2 A. Moving each atom by a full bond length from an
  already-not-minimum geometry (the reactant-TS midpoint) produces
  starting geometries where atoms may overlap.

v5 EXPERIMENTAL REDESIGN:

  Replace the fixed-seed 30-sample experiment with a properly-controlled
  statistical experiment:

  Axis 1: Noise magnitude — {0.5, 1.0, 1.5, 2.0} A (4 levels)
    Maps the difficulty curve. At what noise level does convergence
    break down?

  Axis 2: Noise seed — {42, 123, 456, 789, 1024} (5 seeds)
    Independent noise realizations. Gives mean ± std convergence rates.
    Identifies which failures are seed-dependent vs inherent.

  Axis 3: Optimizer — 4 slim configs (only what works)
    a) SN ε=5e-4:  Best v4 plain config
    b) SN ε=2e-4:  Tied best, slower
    c) SPDN default: tau_hard=0.01, diis_size=8, diis_every=5
    d) SPDN tuned:  tau_hard=0.02, momentum=0.2, diis_every=3

  Total: 4 × 5 × 4 = 80 combinations
  Samples: 50 per combo
  Total trajectories: 4,000

  Directory structure:
    <OUT_DIR>/
      noise_0.5A/
        seed_42/
          mad1.3_se5e-4_pgtrue_phfalse/
          mad1.3_se2e-4_pgtrue_phfalse/
          mad1.3_spdn_pgtrue_phfalse/
          mad1.3_spdn_tuned_pgtrue_phfalse/
        seed_123/
          ...
      noise_1.0A/
        ...

  This design answers:
  1. What is the convergence rate vs noise magnitude curve?
  2. Does SPDN outperform shifted Newton at any noise level?
  3. Are there samples that are universally hard, or does it depend on
     the noise realization?
  4. How robust is each optimizer (low std across seeds = robust)?
  5. Is the 53% ceiling at 2.0A inherent to the problem or specific
     to seed 42?

13. OPEN QUESTIONS & NEXT STEPS
================================================================================

IMMEDIATE NEXT STEP:
  Run the v5 SLURM script. This will produce:
  - Difficulty curve: convergence vs noise magnitude
  - SPDN vs shifted Newton comparison with proper statistics
  - Sample consistency analysis across seeds
  - All diagnostic reports for every noise/seed combination

OPEN QUESTIONS:

  Q1. Does SPDN actually outperform shifted Newton?
      The theoretical arguments are sound (bounded weights, GDIIS for
      oscillation, backtracking for robustness) but we have NO empirical
      evidence yet. The v5 experiment will answer this.

  Q2. What is the natural difficulty curve?
      At 0.5A noise, convergence should be nearly 100%. At 2.0A, it's 53%.
      Where does the transition happen? Is it gradual or a sharp cliff?

  Q3. Are the 7 "impossible" samples (at 2.0A, seed 42) universally hard?
      With 5 seeds, we'll know if they're always hard or just unlucky.
      If they converge with different seeds → noise-dependent.
      If they never converge → inherently hard molecules (or PES problem).

  Q4. Does GDIIS actually fire and help?
      The D3 analysis module will show DIIS attempt/accept rates.
      If accept rate is 0%, the feature is useless. If >10%, it's helping.

  Q5. Is the backtracking line search better than trust region?
      The D4 analysis will show how many backtracks are needed.
      If most steps need 0-1 backtracks, the step quality is good.
      If most need 5+, the step builder needs tuning.

  Q6. What does the PES look like "walking out" from a minimum?
      The D6 analysis traces backward from converged geometries.
      This tells us the basin of attraction radius: how close do you
      need to be for the optimizer to find the minimum?

  Q7. Is the 97% false convergence rate specific to shifted Newton?
      SPDN's modified convergence (n_neg_reliable + force gate) might
      have a much lower false convergence rate. The v5 analysis will
      show.

LONGER-TERM DIRECTIONS:

  - If SPDN works: tune tau_hard and DIIS parameters with the v5 data
  - If SPDN doesn't work: consider switching to L-BFGS or conjugate
    gradient methods that don't need the Hessian
  - Investigate whether DFTB0 is the bottleneck (try DFTB2 or PM6)
  - Consider adaptive noise: start with 0.5A, verify convergence, then
    increase noise to map the basin boundary
  - Multi-start optimization: for hard samples, try 3-5 different noise
    seeds and take the best result
